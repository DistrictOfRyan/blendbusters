How we handle undisclosed doses

Many premium products list a proprietary blend, so the amount of each ingredient is not published. When that happens we cannot verify a dose match, and we say so: the Dose match field reads Data unavailable and does not contribute to the score. A large price gap alone does not make two products equivalent.

Reference sources we consult include the NIH Office of Dietary Supplements, Examine.com, and peer-reviewed human trials (PubMed and Cochrane). Ratings are the BlendBusters desk’s read of that evidence, and are not medical advice.

The ingredient-overlap estimate

Some pages show an estimated ingredient-overlap percentage. It is an estimate of how much two products overlap by active ingredient and intended use, not a lab assay. We match the brand’s disclosed actives to a specific lower-cost product that carries the same or overlapping actives at a comparable dose. Where a per-ingredient dose is hidden behind a proprietary blend, that field is marked Data unavailable and is excluded from dose scoring. The number is a guide to how close the match is, not a guarantee of an identical effect.

Prices & sourcing

Prices are estimates from public retail sources, chiefly Amazon and brand-direct listings, with the date we checked. We normalize to a monthly cost at the compared dose rather than the sticker price. Prices change often, so always verify on the merchant’s site. We cite our sources and date them, and where a specific figure still needs a second editorial check we mark it rather than hide it. We do not copy merchant descriptions; the analysis and calculations are our own.

Editorial standards

  • We label brand claims, BlendBusters analysis, and scientific evidence separately, so you always know who is saying what.
  • We use language like “lower-cost ingredient match,” “overlapping ingredients,” and “similar intended use”, never “exactly the same,” “works just as well,” or “guaranteed equivalent.”
  • We do not fabricate reviews, testimonials, endorsements, test results, prices, or scientific findings.
  • We do not state that a product treats, cures, prevents, or diagnoses any condition.
  • Anyone can send a correction; we date and log every edit.

Affiliate independence

How we make money

BlendBusters earns affiliate commissions when you buy through some merchant links. We don’t manufacture or sell products, purchases happen on third-party merchant sites. Comparisons and verdicts are written before any link is added, and a commission never changes a verdict or a score. We keep a better option in a comparison even when another merchant pays more.
