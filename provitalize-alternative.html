What matches

  • ✓Overlapping ingredients — both are multi-strain oral probiotic capsules using Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium genera with a high disclosed total CFU (68B vs 60B).
  • ✓A similar intended use — daily gut / digestive support.
  • ✓Physician’s Choice 60 Billion is a named, lower-cost ingredient match on the probiotic base.

What differs

  • ≠Strains differ — Provitalize uses named strains (L. gasseri SBT-2055, B. breve IDCC 4401, B. lactis R101-8); probiotics are strain-specific, so effects aren’t interchangeable.
  • ≠Provitalize adds turmeric, moringa, curry leaf, and BioPerine the match doesn’t replicate (the match adds a prebiotic fiber instead).
  • ≠Provitalize is DTC/subscription-priced; the match is widely retail-available.

Safety & who should check with a professional

Safety note

Turmeric and BioPerine can affect the absorption of some medications, and turmeric may matter for those with gallbladder issues or on blood thinners.
