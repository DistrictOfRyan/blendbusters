🔥 Real talk: what actually works

  • Phosphatidylserine is the one real active — but at 100mg it’s below the ~300mg used in most studies. A generic bottle lets you dose it right.
  • The coffee-cherry extract (Neurofactor) has weak evidence — it raises a blood marker (BDNF), not proven memory or focus.
  • The Plus version bolts on three B vitamins worth about two dollars a month — no benefit unless you’re deficient.
  • A generic PS at 100mg is pennies a serving, and a B-complex is a few dollars for months.
  • You’re paying a brand premium for two commodity ingredients you can buy separately for a third of the price.
